# AQA GCSE Religious Studies (8062, Spec A) — Curriculum Overview

## Assessment Structure

- **Paper 1** (1h 45m, 96 marks): The study of religions — beliefs, teachings, and practices
  - Section A: Christianity (48 marks)
  - Section B: Islam (48 marks)
- **Paper 2** (1h 45m, 96 marks): Thematic studies
  - Four themes from A–F, each worth 24 marks
  - Student studies all six themes A–F

**Assessment objectives (applied equally across both papers):**
- AO1 (50%): Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of religions, beliefs, teachings, practices, sources of wisdom and authority
- AO2 (50%): Analyse and evaluate aspects of religion and belief, including their significance and influence

## Question Structure

Each question on the exam paper follows a five-part structure:

| Part | Marks | Type | AO |
|------|-------|------|-----|
| (a) | 1 | Give one... | AO1 |
| (b) | 2 | Give two... | AO1 |
| (c) | 4 | Explain two... | AO1 |
| (d) | 5 | Explain two... with reference to a source of wisdom and authority | AO1 |
| (e) | 12 (+3 SPaG) | Evaluate — "X." Discuss this statement... | AO2 |

## Topic Map

### Paper 1 — The Study of Religions

#### 1. Christianity: Beliefs and Teachings
- 1.1 The nature of God — omnipotent, omnibenevolent, just; the Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)
- 1.2 God as a Trinity — three persons, one God; significance for Christians today
- 1.3 Creation — Genesis 1–3; role of Word and Spirit; stewardship
- 1.4 The Incarnation — Jesus as God in human form; John 1:1–14
- 1.5 The Crucifixion — events, significance, atonement
- 1.6 The Resurrection and Ascension — Gospel and Acts accounts, significance for Christians today
- 1.7 Afterlife and judgement — heaven, hell, purgatory (Catholic), resurrection of the body; parable of the Sheep and Goats (Matthew 25)
- 1.8 Sin and salvation — original sin, redemption through Christ, grace, the role of the Holy Spirit

#### 2. Christianity: Practices
- 2.1 Worship — liturgical, non-liturgical, informal; significance and differences
- 2.2 Prayer — the Lord's Prayer, set prayers, informal prayer; importance for Christians
- 2.3 Sacraments — baptism (infant and believers'), Holy Communion (Eucharist); significance and differences between denominations
- 2.4 Pilgrimage — Lourdes, Iona; purposes and significance
- 2.5 Celebrations — Christmas and Easter; significance and how they are celebrated
- 2.6 The role of the Church in the local community — food banks, worship, education, social activities
- 2.7 The place of mission and evangelism — the Great Commission (Matthew 28:16–20)
- 2.8 Church growth — worldwide growth and persecution; ecumenical movement; reconciliation

#### 3. Islam: Beliefs and Teachings
- 3.1 The Six Articles of Faith (Sunni) and Five Roots of Usul ad-Din (Shi'a)
- 3.2 Tawhid — oneness of God; Surah 112 (Al-Ikhlas)
- 3.3 Angels — Jibril, Mika'il, Israfil; role in communicating God's message
- 3.4 Predestination (al-Qadr) — God's omniscience; free will and human responsibility
- 3.5 Akhirah — life after death, resurrection, judgement, al-Jannah (paradise), Jahannam (hell)
- 3.6 Prophethood (Risalah) — Adam, Ibrahim, Muhammad; the Seal of the Prophets
- 3.7 The holy books — Qur'an as the literal word of God; Torah, Psalms, Gospels; Hadith and Sunnah

#### 4. Islam: Practices
- 4.1 The Five Pillars — Shahadah, Salah, Zakah, Sawm, Hajj; their significance
- 4.2 The Ten Obligatory Acts (Shi'a) — including Khums and Jihad
- 4.3 Salah — preparation (wudu), rak'ahs, significance of set prayer; Jummah prayers
- 4.4 Sawm — fasting during Ramadan; rules, exemptions, significance; Night of Power (Laylat al-Qadr)
- 4.5 Zakah and Khums — giving 2.5%; Khums (20%) in Shi'a Islam; purposes
- 4.6 Hajj — the pilgrimage to Makkah; significance; key rituals (Tawaf, Sa'y, Arafat, Mina)
- 4.7 Jihad — greater jihad (spiritual struggle) and lesser jihad (just war conditions); widely misunderstood
- 4.8 Festivals — Eid ul-Adha, Eid ul-Fitr, Ashura; how they are celebrated and their significance

### Paper 2 — Thematic Studies

#### 5. Theme A: Relationships and Families
- 5.1 Sexual relationships — sex before marriage (fornication), sex outside marriage (adultery)
- 5.2 Contraception — religious attitudes; Catholic natural law vs other Christian views; Islamic views
- 5.3 Marriage — nature and purpose; religious ceremonies; inter-faith marriage
- 5.4 Divorce and remarriage — religious attitudes; Catholic annulment; Islamic divorce
- 5.5 The nature and purpose of families — nuclear, extended, single-parent; procreation, stability, education
- 5.6 Gender equality — roles of men and women in religion and society; changing attitudes

#### 6. Theme B: Religion and Life
- 6.1 Origins of the universe — religious and scientific explanations; compatibility/conflict
- 6.2 The value of the world — stewardship and dominion; Khalifah
- 6.3 Use and abuse of the environment — pollution, deforestation, natural resources
- 6.4 Use and abuse of animals — factory farming, animal testing, vegetarianism
- 6.5 Origins of human life — evolution and creationism; religious responses to science
- 6.6 Abortion — sanctity of life, quality of life, religious and non-religious arguments
- 6.7 Euthanasia — active, passive, voluntary; religious and ethical perspectives

#### 7. Theme C: The Existence of God and Revelation
- 7.1 Design argument — Paley's watch; strengths and weaknesses
- 7.2 First Cause argument — cosmological argument; everything has a cause
- 7.3 The existence of suffering — problem of evil; inconsistent triad; theodicies
- 7.4 Visions and miracles — examples, significance, challenges
- 7.5 Religious experience — numinous, conversion, prayer; value as evidence
- 7.6 General and special revelation — scripture, nature, prophets, enlightenment
- 7.7 Arguments against the existence of God — science, suffering, lack of evidence

#### 8. Theme D: Religion, Peace and Conflict
- 8.1 Violence and terrorism — religious and non-religious attitudes
- 8.2 Reasons for war — self-defence, greed, resources, retaliation
- 8.3 The Just War theory — Christian criteria (Aquinas); lesser jihad conditions in Islam
- 8.4 Holy war — crusades, concept of fighting for God
- 8.5 Pacifism — Quakers, conscientious objectors, Martin Luther King Jr
- 8.6 Weapons of mass destruction — nuclear weapons; religious attitudes
- 8.7 The role of religion in peacemaking — religious organisations, peace movements, forgiveness and reconciliation

#### 9. Theme E: Religion, Crime and Punishment
- 9.1 Reasons for crime — poverty, upbringing, mental illness, greed, opposition to unjust laws
- 9.2 Religious attitudes to lawbreakers — forgiveness, justice, reform
- 9.3 Three aims of punishment — retribution, deterrence, reformation
- 9.4 Religious attitudes to suffering — redemptive suffering, punishment from God, test of faith
- 9.5 Treatment of criminals — prison, community service; religious attitudes to corporal punishment
- 9.6 Forgiveness — religious teachings on forgiveness; when forgiveness is difficult
- 9.7 The death penalty — religious and non-religious arguments for and against

#### 10. Theme F: Religion, Human Rights and Social Justice
- 10.1 Human rights — UN Declaration, religious attitudes to rights and responsibilities
- 10.2 Prejudice and discrimination — racism, sexism, religious discrimination; religious teachings
- 10.3 Religious freedom — right to practise, convert, express beliefs; limits of tolerance
- 10.4 Prejudice and discrimination in religion — role of women, attitudes to homosexuality
- 10.5 Wealth and poverty — causes of poverty; religious teachings on wealth; responsible use of money
- 10.6 Exploitation of the poor — fair trade, debt, religious responses
- 10.7 Charity — Christian and Islamic teachings on giving; the work of religious charities (Christian Aid, Islamic Relief)
